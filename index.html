Stirling Project
  • –
    This project aimed to study the possibility of providing, the market in need of a self-sufficient eletrictal power source, a better solution than fossil fuel generators by using the sun provided heat.

    In this effort, there was developed a engine capable of converting heat flux in mechanical energy or vice versa. By providing the mechanical energy that can be converted to electrical power, this engine can be used along with photovoltaic modules. And by receiving mechanical power, this engine is able to create enough of a difference in thermal energy to liquify the air in contact with it.

    Thus, in one hand, this engine can use the accumulated heat that diminishes photovoltaic modules efficiency; and in the other hand it is proving useful as a refrigerator resource to COVID19 vaccines cooling.

    In this project me and my team researched the mathematics of the stirling thermodynamic cycle in order to identify optimal key parameters and structural configurations that would better attend the torque and rotation speed requirements to produce at least 1200W of eletrical power.

    Once found those parameters were used to projected the engine structure trying to minimize potential issues with gas leaking and vibration. And based on the projected geometry we ran proofs of concept finit element analisys simulations to refine the project.
